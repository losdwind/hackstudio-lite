- **Key milestones**:
  - 2021: Started exporting Tang EV to Norway

## Xiaomi "Person-Car-Home" (ren che jia) Ecosystem

### Official Name
- Chinese: **"ren che jia quan sheng tai"** (ren-che-jia full ecosystem)
- English: **"Human x Car x Home"** or **"People-Car-Home Full Ecosystem"**

### Announcement & Context
- **October 26, 2023**: Xiaomi unveiled the "ren che jia quan sheng tai" strategy alongside the launch of **Xiaomi HyperOS** (replacing MIUI)
- This marked Xiaomi's strategic upgrade from a smartphone/IoT company to a fully integrated smart living company
- HyperOS unifies MIUI, Vela (IoT OS), Mina, and automotive OS into a single platform

### Key Ecosystem Details

**Scale (as of Dec 2024):**
- 904.6 million connected IoT devices (excluding phones/tablets/laptops)
- Over 200 product categories
- 5,000+ R&D personnel developed HyperOS over 7 years
- System designed to connect up to 8.2 billion device endpoints

**Integration Features:**
- Seamless connectivity between Xiaomi phones and SU7 car system
- Xiao Ai voice assistant works across phone, car, and home devices
- Smart home devices controllable from car dashboard
- Phone-as-key for SU7
- Navigation seamlessly transfers between phone and car
- Highway toll QR code payment via car's Xiao Ai assistant
- Home appliance control from vehicle (e.g., turn on AC before arriving home)

### Lei Jun Quotes on the Ecosystem
- **"This is a strategy [Xiaomi] absolutely cannot abandon."** -- Lei Jun on the ren-che-jia strategy
- **"Intelligence and ecology are Xiaomi's core strengths."** -- Lei Jun
- **"Devotedly build a good car for Mi fans."** -- Lei Jun at SU7 launch
- At the SU7 launch, Lei Jun expressed **"unwavering confidence in victory"**
- Lei Jun described entering the auto industry as his **"last major entrepreneurial project"** and vowed to stake **"all reputation and achievements"** on it

## Number of Chinese Tech Companies Entering Auto Industry

### Confirmed Tech-to-Auto Entrants

| Company | Entry Type | Details |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Xiaomi** | Direct manufacturer | Only Chinese tech company doing full design, development, manufacturing |
| **Huawei** | Technology partner (3 models) | HI mode, Harmony Intelligent Mobility Alliance (HIMA), parts supplier |
| **Baidu** | Autonomous driving + JiDU brand | Apollo autonomous driving platform; JiDU Auto (joint venture with Geely) |
| **Qihoo 360** | Investor | Invested in Hozon Auto (Neta brand) |
| **Alibaba** | Investor + technology | Invested in Xpeng; collaborated on smart cockpit tech |
| **Tencent** | Investor | Invested in NIO and various EV startups |
| **Didi** | Joint venture (abandoned) | Brief foray with BYD; largely exited |
| **Foxconn (Hon Hai)** | Contract manufacturing | Foxtron brand; manufacturing platform for EVs |

**Total count**: At least **6-8 major tech companies** have entered the EV space in some capacity (Xiaomi, Huawei, Baidu, 360, Alibaba, Tencent, Didi, Foxconn). Xiaomi is unique as the only one doing full direct manufacturing.

### Combined Investment
- Huawei, Baidu, and Xiaomi alone invested a combined **$19 billion** in EV ventures and self-driving technology (as reported by TechTimes, May 2021)

### Contrast with Apple
- Apple's car project (Project Titan) was cancelled in February 2024 after 10 years and reportedly $10 billion+ spent
- Meanwhile, Chinese tech companies (Xiaomi, Huawei) successfully launched vehicles
- InsideEVs headline: "The Apple Car Failed But China's Huawei And Xiaomi Are Winning"
