## Xiaomi's Total Car Investment Commitment

### Clarification on the Numbers
- **Script says "650 billion yuan / 100 billion yuan"** -- This needs correction:
  - The **100 billion yuan (10 billion USD)** figure is CORRECT. On March 30, 2021, Xiaomi announced an initial investment of **10 billion RMB**, with an expected total investment of **$10 billion USD (~65-70 billion RMB) over 10 years**.
  - The **650 billion yuan** figure does NOT appear in any verified source. This may be a misquotation or confusion with other numbers.
  - A separate, later commitment: Lei Jun announced "at least 200 billion yuan in R&D over the next five years" (average 40 billion yuan/year), but this covers all Xiaomi R&D, not just automotive.

### Verified Investment Timeline
| Date | Amount | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| March 30, 2021 | 10 billion RMB (initial) | First phase automotive investment |
| 10-year plan | $10 billion USD total | Long-term automotive commitment |
| By Dec 2023 | >10 billion RMB in R&D | First car (SU7) development cost alone |
| 2025 results | 106.1 billion RMB | Automotive + innovation revenue (not investment) |

## Lei Jun's 75-Day Research Trip

### Verified Details
- **Duration**: 75 days (some sources say "over 70 days")
- **Visits**: 85 visits conducted
- **Interviews**: 200+ industry insiders/experts consulted
- **Cities visited**: More than 10 cities
- **Board meetings**: 4 executive meetings held during research period
- **Research team**: 6-person team initially formed

### Context & Timeline
- January 2021: Xiaomi faces US sanctions (placed on Entity List), triggering strategic rethink
- Jan-Mar 2021: Lei Jun conducts the 75-day research trip
- Initially met with EV startups and autonomous vehicle makers in which Xiaomi or Lei had invested
- March 30, 2021: Official announcement -- Lei Jun declared he would stake "all reputation and achievements" on this venture
- Lei Jun said this would be his "last major entrepreneurial project"

## Talent Flow from Tech Companies to EV (2020-2021)

### Key Data Points
- The NEV industry saw massive talent inflow during 2020-2021
- BYD R&D personnel increased 66% from 2021 to 2022, with R&D team representing 12.2% of total workforce
- 73% of auto-tech firms reported difficulty hiring senior software and battery engineers (as of 2025)
- Highest talent gaps: AI, sensor fusion, battery optimization, data security
- Xiaomi's first car involved **3,400 engineers** in R&D

### Tech Company-to-EV Talent Movement [UNVERIFIED -- specific flow numbers not found in public sources]
- Major tech companies (Baidu, Huawei, ByteDance, Alibaba) saw significant talent outflow to EV startups
- Autonomous driving engineers were among the most sought-after
- Salary premiums of 30-50% were common for tech talent moving to EV companies [UNVERIFIED]

## Comparison: NIO, XPeng, Li Auto Initial Funding

| Company | Founded | Pre-IPO Funding | IPO | IPO Amount |
|---------|---------|-----------------|-----|------------|
| NIO | 2014 | >$2.4 billion (6 rounds, 50+ investors) | Sept 2018 (NYSE) | ~$1 billion |
| XPeng | 2014 | ~$900 million+ (including $500M + $400M rounds pre-IPO) | Aug 2020 (NYSE) | $1.5 billion |
| Li Auto | 2015 | ~$1.5 billion+ (including $530M Series C in Aug 2019) | July 2020 (Nasdaq) | $1.1 billion (+$380M private placement) |
| **Xiaomi Auto** | **2021** | **$10 billion USD committed (10-year plan)** | **N/A (subsidiary of listed Xiaomi)** | **N/A** |

### Key Difference
Xiaomi entered with significantly more capital backing than any of the "new forces" (NIO/XPeng/Li Auto) had at their founding. As a profitable, publicly-traded company with >$10B cash reserves, Xiaomi did not need external VC funding for its auto division.
