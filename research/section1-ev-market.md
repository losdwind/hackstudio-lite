# Section 1 -- China EV Market + Xiaomi Background

## China NEV Penetration Rate by Year

| Year | Penetration Rate | Source/Notes |
|------|-----------------|--------------|
| 2019 | ~4.7% | Based on 1.206M NEV sales out of ~25.8M total vehicle sales. Wikipedia (Plug-in EVs in China) reports 4.2% for plug-in passenger cars specifically. [APPROXIMATE -- exact figure varies by source: 4.2%-5.0% depending on whether all NEVs or only plug-in passenger cars are counted] |
| 2020 | ~5.4% | Based on 1.367M NEV sales. Multiple sources confirm ~5.4% range. |
| 2021 | ~13.4% | Confirmed by multiple sources including Statista (BEV share 10.9%, total NEV ~13.4%). Major inflection point year. |
| 2022 | ~25.6% | CAAM data; some sources report 26%. NEV sales reached 6.886M units. |
| 2023 | 31.6% | Confirmed by CAAM. NEV production 9.587M, sales 9.495M, up 37.9% YoY. |
| 2024 | ~47.9% | Confirmed by multiple sources. Some report 40.9% (CAAM wholesale basis including exports) vs. ~47.9% (retail/domestic basis). NEV sales reached 12.866M total. |

**Key narrative**: Penetration went from ~5% to ~48% in just five years (2019-2024), one of the fastest technology adoption curves in automotive history.

## Xiaomi Key Milestones

### Founding & Early History
- **Founded**: April 6, 2010, in Beijing
- **Founders**: Lei Jun, Lin Bin, Zhou Guangping, Liu De, Li Wanqiang, Wong Kong Kat, Hong Feng (7 co-founders)
- **First product**: MIUI custom Android ROM (2010); Mi 1 smartphone launched August 2011
- **2014**: Largest smartphone market share in China; valuation reached ~$45 billion

### IPO
- **July 9, 2018**: Listed on Hong Kong Stock Exchange (1810.HK)
- Lei Jun held roughly 30% of shares at IPO
